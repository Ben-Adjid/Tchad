Lesson 4: Days of the Week and Time

In this lesson, you will learn how to say the days of the week and tell time in Chadian Arabic.

Days of the Week

Here are the days of the week in Chadian Arabic:

  • Monday: الاثنين (Al-Ithnayn)
  • Tuesday: الثلاثاء (Ath-Thulatha)
  • Wednesday: الأربعاء (Al-Arba'a)
  • Thursday: الخميس (Al-Khamis)
  • Friday: الجمعة (Al-Jum'a)
  • Saturday: السبت (As-Sabt)
  • Sunday: الأحد (Al-Ahad)

Months of the Year

Here are the months of the year in Chadian Arabic:

  • January: يناير (Yanayir)
  • February: فبراير (Febrayir)
  • March: مارس (Mars)
  • April: أبريل (Abril)
  • May: مايو (Mayo)
  • June: يونيو (Yunyu)
  • July: يوليو (Yulyu)
  • August: أغسطس (Aghustus)
  • September: سبتمبر (Sibtambar)
  • October: أكتوبر (Uktubar)
  • November: نوفمبر (Nufambar)
  • December: ديسمبر (Disambar)

Telling Time

Here are some phrases to help you tell time in Chadian Arabic:

  • What time is it?: كم الساعة؟ (Kam as-sa'a?)
  • It is [hour]: الساعة [hour] (As-sa'a [hour])
  • Half past: والنصف (Wa an-nisf)
  • Quarter past: والربع (Wa ar-rub')
  • Quarter to: إلا الربع (Illa ar-rub')

Examples:

  • It is 3 o'clock: الساعة الثالثة (As-sa'a ath-thalitha)
  • It is half past 4: الساعة الرابعة والنصف (As-sa'a ar-rabi'a wa an-nisf)
  • It is quarter past 5: الساعة الخامسة والربع (As-sa'a al-khamisa wa ar-rub')
  • It is quarter to 6: الساعة السادسة إلا الربع (As-sa'a as-sadisa illa ar-rub')

Practice Exercises

Practice saying the days of the week and telling time in Chadian Arabic. Here are some exercises to help you get started:

  • Say the days of the week in order.
  • Practice saying the current day of the week.
  • Practice telling the current time.
  • Ask a friend or family member what time it is and respond in Chadian Arabic.

Practice these exercises regularly to become more comfortable with using days of the week and telling time in Chadian Arabic. In the next lesson, we will cover basic grammar and sentence structure in Chadian Arabic.
