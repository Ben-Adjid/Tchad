Lesson 2: Basic Greetings and Phrases

In this lesson, you will learn how to greet people and use common phrases in Chadian Arabic.

Common Greetings

Greetings are an essential part of any language. Here are some common greetings in Chadian Arabic:

  • Hello: مرحبا (Marhaba)
  • Good morning: صباح الخير (Sabah al-khair)
  • Good afternoon: مساء الخير (Masa' al-khair)
  • Good evening: مساء الخير (Masa' al-khair)
  • Good night: تصبح على خير (Tusbih ala khair)
  • Goodbye: مع السلامة (Ma'a as-salama)

Polite Expressions

Using polite expressions can make your interactions more pleasant. Here are some polite expressions in Chadian Arabic:

  • Please: من فضلك (Min fadlak)
  • Thank you: شكرا (Shukran)
  • You're welcome: عفوا (Afwan)
  • Excuse me: عفوا (Afwan)
  • Sorry: آسف (Asif)

Basic Conversational Phrases

Here are some basic conversational phrases to help you start a conversation in Chadian Arabic:

  • How are you?: كيف حالك؟ (Kayfa halak?)
  • I am fine: أنا بخير (Ana bikhair)
  • What is your name?: ما اسمك؟ (Ma ismuk?)
  • My name is [Name]: اسمي [Name] (Ismi [Name])
  • Where are you from?: من أين أنت؟ (Min ayna anta?)
  • I am from [Country]: أنا من [Country] (Ana min [Country])

Practice Dialogues

Here are some practice dialogues to help you get comfortable with using these greetings and phrases:

Dialogue 1: Meeting Someone for the First Time

Person A: مرحبا (Marhaba) - Hello

Person B: مرحبا (Marhaba) - Hello

Person A: كيف حالك؟ (Kayfa halak?) - How are you?

Person B: أنا بخير، شكرا. وأنت؟ (Ana bikhair, shukran. Wa anta?) - I am fine, thank you. And you?

Person A: أنا بخير. ما اسمك؟ (Ana bikhair. Ma ismuk?) - I am fine. What is your name?

Person B: اسمي [Name]. وأنت؟ (Ismi [Name]. Wa anta?) - My name is [Name]. And you?

Person A: اسمي [Name]. (Ismi [Name].) - My name is [Name].

Dialogue 2: Asking for Directions

Person A: عفوا، أين السوق؟ (Afwan, ayna as-suq?) - Excuse me, where is the market?

Person B: السوق هناك. (As-suq hunak.) - The market is over there.

Person A: شكرا جزيلا. (Shukran jazeelan.) - Thank you very much.

Person B: عفوا. (Afwan.) - You're welcome.

Practice these dialogues with a partner or in front of a mirror to build your confidence in using Chadian Arabic greetings and phrases. In the next lesson, we will cover numbers and counting in Chadian Arabic.
