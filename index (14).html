Lesson 3: Numbers and Counting

In this lesson, you will learn how to count and use numbers in Chadian Arabic.

Numbers 1-10

Here are the numbers from 1 to 10 in Chadian Arabic:

  • 1: واحد (Wahid)
  • 2: اثنان (Ithnan)
  • 3: ثلاثة (Thalatha)
  • 4: أربعة (Arba'a)
  • 5: خمسة (Khamsa)
  • 6: ستة (Sitta)
  • 7: سبعة (Sab'a)
  • 8: ثمانية (Thamaniya)
  • 9: تسعة (Tis'a)
  • 10: عشرة (Ashara)

Numbers 11-20

Here are the numbers from 11 to 20 in Chadian Arabic:

  • 11: أحد عشر (Ahad ashar)
  • 12: اثنا عشر (Ithna ashar)
  • 13: ثلاثة عشر (Thalatha ashar)
  • 14: أربعة عشر (Arba'a ashar)
  • 15: خمسة عشر (Khamsa ashar)
  • 16: ستة عشر (Sitta ashar)
  • 17: سبعة عشر (Sab'a ashar)
  • 18: ثمانية عشر (Thamaniya ashar)
  • 19: تسعة عشر (Tis'a ashar)
  • 20: عشرون (Ishrun)

Counting Objects

When counting objects in Chadian Arabic, the number usually comes before the noun. Here are some examples:

  • One apple: تفاحة واحدة (Tuffaha wahida)
  • Two books: كتابان (Kitaban)
  • Three cars: ثلاث سيارات (Thalath sayarat)
  • Four houses: أربعة بيوت (Arba'a buyut)
  • Five pens: خمسة أقلام (Khamsa aqlam)

Practice Exercises

Practice counting objects around you in Chadian Arabic. Here are some exercises to help you get started:

  • Count the number of chairs in your room.
  • Count the number of books on your shelf.
  • Count the number of windows in your house.
  • Count the number of fruits in your kitchen.
  • Count the number of cars on your street.

Practice these exercises regularly to become more comfortable with using numbers in Chadian Arabic. In the next lesson, we will cover the days of the week and telling time in Chadian Arabic.
